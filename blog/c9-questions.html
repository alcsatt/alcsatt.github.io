Asking Questions

What Makes a Good One

May 16, 2015

Although I haven't actually asked any questions on our Google+ group -- one word immediately comes to mind: specificity. The more specific a question is, the more likely I am to both read it and replicate it. By taking the time to make a question concise and specific, it shows that the asker values both his time and the time of the people who may potentially help him. Questions usually come up as a result of confusion or a problem that one is having. Something important to include in your question is the context. The more the asker can describe their situation or their line of thinking -- the better chance that somebody will be able to guide you in the proper direction. Explaining what you were doing when you encountered a problem and any of the roadblocks that prevented you from solving the problem really help to paint a bigger picture. The problem may stem from a lack of understanding in another area as opposed to one related to that particular question.

This may be a short post, but asking good questions really comes down to respect for your potential readers. Try to be as clear and concise as possible. Utilize proper grammar and punctuation. Always be specific.

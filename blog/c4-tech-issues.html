Becoming Obsolete

A Business' Worst Nightmare

April 12, 2015

When one thinks about problems facing the tech world -- my head immediately goes to security issues because that is where my main interests lie. Some of the biggest news stories over the last few years are rooted in how we're being constantly surveilled or our personal information is under attack. However, my goal is to focus on something that I usually don't pay attention to.

The threat of becoming obsolete is something that businesses (especially in the tech world) constantly have to guard against or prepare for. This article really peaked my interest in this issue. It brings up many valid examples of technologies that used to be standards in the tech industry, but now things have changed. I particularly liked the example of data storage media. The picture in the article shows the progression of floppy disks. If you grew up in the 1990's like me then all you really remember is the smallest floppy disk before it was eventually replaced by the CD. Another niche technology that the article brings up which were huge at the time were MP3 players. I remember when those first came out. Everyone though it was incredible. You could store so many songs on them and they were only a few button pushes away. Now, most people use their mobile phones instead of having a device dedicated to just playing music.

I could go on about all the examples that the article brings up, but I want to bring up the real open question: How does one avoid becoming obsolete?
I don't really have an answer for that, and I'm not sure anyone else has a definitive formula for it either. However, there are tech companies innovating all over the world. In my opinion, the best way to avoid obsoletion is to keep innovating and to keep pushing yourself. Thinking that you have an unbeatable product or technology leads to complacency. Look at companies like Apple. They always seem to not only be ahead of the curve, but they set the curve. Their philosophy centers around encouraging different schools of thought. If everyone did things the same way, the world would be a pretty boring place.

Do I have all the answers to this? Absolutely not. I just believe it's a perpetual and pervasive issue that everyone must consider.

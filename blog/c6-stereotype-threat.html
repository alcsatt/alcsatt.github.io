Stereotype Threats

April 26, 2015

Stereotype threats are a very interesting notion that I've never actually had explicitly defined. You can go in to more detail about them here. They basically exist because someone is afraid of doing something that would confirm a negative stereotype about themselves. I saw how this would apply to other groups (particularly races) immediately, but as a white male in his twenties, I had a little more trouble thinking about it. After some thought, I feel like I've come up with something.

In the past, I've been afraid of underperforming in academics and athletics because of a stereotype that most white males are privileged. I remember consciouslly thinking to myself in some situations: "People are going to look down on me if I don't go to a great college after getting so many opportunities and going to great prep schools growing up." It's a constant fear -- not being good enough and being worried about what other people think of me. I don't expect the fear to ever go away, but I do expect myself to have the courage to overcome it. When I do that, I'll be a better person. This is particularly important in a program like DBC. I come from a background in Computer Science so I definitely have some fears about how people will perceive me if I don't know the answer to a problem. I feel like people assume that anyone who has a Computer Science degree should just fly right through all this material. If I worry about that, it will become a self-fulfilling prophecy. If I don't know something and someone else does, I'll learn from it. The next time the same problem comes up, I'll make it my goal to know the answer. In this way, I hope to provide a good example for others. I need to own my knowledge and use it to help teach people.
